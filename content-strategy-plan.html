01 · Why now

The office is being argued about at the dinner table

For fifty years, nobody talked about offices. Now everyone does. These are real stories, running right now — click any of them:

The share of Fortune 100 companies requiring full-time in-person work jumped from 5% to 54% since 2023 (JLL, via Built In) — while Stanford's survey of 1,000+ executives found only 12% plan any RTO mandate, and workers say they'd rather quit than fully return. Both things are true at once. That contradiction is a content goldmine, and it's being argued in living rooms, not boardrooms.

Cost certainty in an uncertain environment

The U.S. and Canadian office markets are recovering, with availability falling for seven consecutive quarters, but the ground-up construction cycle that would have supplied new inventory has not returned. Renovation and lease renewal investment now constitute the primary pathway for occupiers to establish competitive workplaces.

Key highlights

The cost floor is set to rise with the prevalence of AI. Technology infrastructure requirements embedded in a standard corporate fit-out are categorically more complex and expensive than just a few years ago. MEP and AV/IT together represent a growing share of total fit-out cost, reaching up to 12% in some markets, and that share will grow as technical workplace specifications become the norm.

Cost growth is directionally consistent but locally variable. The spread between the highest- and lowest-cost markets has widened in 2026, reflecting increasingly divergent labor availability and demand conditions. A single average figure obscures variation in mechanisms and trajectories that are consequential for capital planning.

10 · Journey fit

This platform is the marketing layer of the customer journey

The GTM Backbone runs one 8-phase lifecycle — a loop, not a funnel, anchored to T-0, the order date. The buying process only occupies phases 2–4; the recurring-revenue thesis lives in phases 5–8. Every piece of this content plan has exactly one home in that loop.

The journey's Layer 3 already asks for this platform by name: phase 1 calls for an "owned media program to workplace decision-makers" — that's the four pillars. Phase 2's lead campaign is the "Workplace Scorecard diagnostic offer" — that's our tool. Phase 7 wants "quarterly insight reports" and asset-age refresh campaigns — that's The Research and Gear. This plan isn't a second strategy to reconcile with the journey; it's the delivery mechanism for the journey's marketing layer. The journey decides when each asset fires and at whom — the platform is what fires.
